Project #3 Experimental Clock -9/21/2021

Design Process

I feel time differently in my day. It also refreshes at 1 am everyday, when I usually go to bed. It goes fast when I’m asleep or chilling; it goes s…l…o…w… when I have too much to do or studying. For example, 1-2am is slow and tortured, because everything happened on that day would quickly run again in my mind, when I also try to fall asleep. And when I’m finally asleep, time just become fuzzy and chaotic for the next few hours. Or, I always feel time is super long in the morning, that I could complete so many tasks and it would still be in the morning. But sometimes it feels too slow that I am drowning, especially when class is going on and on and there’s no time for lunch. Thus, I drew this clock to visualize my flow in a day. I condensed my 24 hour into 129 second, and I hope you could feel the time with me.

Reflection

I chose my clock out from these two other options. One is my meal-reminder-box, that I view my day in all different kinds of meal. The other one is a visualization of how time disappears that all these bars representing hour, minute, second would just fade away.

I ended up with my melting clock because I wanted to record my feelings about how my schedule and rhythm has changed after the school starts, and my struggles of adjusting the new flow.
In order to code the time circles for my clock, I was experimenting with millies(), and I had to test a lot of times just by re-play the animation and wait, to see if it’s landed with the exact number. However, I think there must be a smarter way.
